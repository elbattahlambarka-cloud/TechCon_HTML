Our History

TechCon began in 2010 as a small gathering of technology enthusiasts in a community center. What started as a modest event with just 200 attendees has now grown into one of the most anticipated technology conferences globally.

Over the years, TechCon has evolved to keep pace with the rapidly changing tech landscape. From the early days focusing on web development and emerging social media platforms, we've expanded to cover artificial intelligence, blockchain, quantum computing, and other cutting-edge technologies.

By 2018, TechCon had outgrown its original venue and moved to the Metropolitan Convention Center, where we now host over 10,000 attendees annually. Our journey reflects the explosive growth of the technology sector itself.

Our Mission

At TechCon, our mission is to foster innovation, education, and collaboration within the global technology community. We believe that by bringing together diverse minds and perspectives, we can accelerate progress and create technology that benefits all of humanity.

We are committed to:

  • Providing a platform for sharing groundbreaking research and ideas
  • Creating opportunities for meaningful networking and collaboration
  • Promoting diversity and inclusion in the tech industry
  • Inspiring the next generation of technologists and innovators
  • Addressing the ethical implications of emerging technologies

Notable Past Speakers

TechCon has been privileged to host some of the most influential minds in technology. Here are just a few of the remarkable speakers who have graced our stages in previous years:

Dr. Aisha Chen

Pioneer in ethical artificial intelligence and machine learning. Head of AI Research at NeuroTech, Dr. Chen presented her groundbreaking work on bias detection in algorithms at TechCon 2022.

Marcus Johnson

Lead architect of the open-source Neptune framework. His 2021 talk on scalable cloud infrastructure revolutionized how startups approach system design.

Sarah El-Masri

World-renowned cybersecurity expert who exposed critical vulnerabilities in global banking systems. Her 2020 presentation led to industry-wide security reforms.

David Kim

Quantum computing researcher whose work at TechCon 2019 demonstrated the first practical application of quantum algorithms in climate modeling.
